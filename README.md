**Project Title:** A2_Group2A_MidTermGame
**Group Number:** 2A
**Description:**
**_Game’s concept_**
A personal shopper is assigned to shop for an individual, given three specific items they must locate and collect within a set time limit.
Mechanics:
Our mechanics are the player can move through the grocery store, find items and add to the shopping cart, complete the task before time runs out, and there is a legend at the side of the screen, which tells the player what items they need to get

**_Game affordances_**
In our game, the perceived affordances is that the player may think they can select any shelf item that resembles a required product. However, the actual affordance is that the player can only select the exact matching item based on it’s colour, strokes, and shape. The game also gives clear instructions to the player on which keys should be used to move around the grocery story and instructs the player to click the item they wish to select. Players are also given hints, which makes the product glow so they know what to collect.

**_Player experience_**
We designed our game to make players feel excited when choosing who they are shopping for, while also experiencing a sense of urgency from the time limit. This encourages players to stay focused and be careful with their choices, since selecting the wrong item causes them to lose a life.
We wanted the player to notice the difficulty of selecting the correct product when items appear in a low-contrast state, making them harder to distinguish. The player should also recognize that they can move move throughout the store to retrieve items from different aisles, reinforcing the feeling of navigating a grocery environment. Additionally, the time limit creates a pressure to make quicker decisions, which both highlights how grocery shopping with low vision can be challenging and keeps the gameplay fast-paced and engaging.
Instead of directly explaining the disability, we demonstrate it through gameplay. Everyday tasks like grocery shopping can be challenging for individuals with low vision, especially when trying to distinguish between items that look similar in colour or when important details are written in very small text.
We avoided pity or disability tourism by focusing on a common, everyday experience rather than portraying the disability as tragic or exaggerated. Instead, the gameplay helps players understand some of the practical challenges of living with low vision in a respectful and engaging way.

**Setup and Interaction Instructions:** You are up for a promotion, but only if you can accurately collect all the required items within one minute. Your task is to gather the exact number of items displayed at the top center of the screen.
Use the arrow keys to move your character and click your mouse to select an item.
You have two hints per level. When used, a hint will highlight the items you need to collect. However, each hint will reduce your remaining time.

**Iteration Notes**

**Post-Playtest:**
**_Change 1:_** We received feedback during playtesting that the shelves were not well organized and didn’t represent a grocery store well. To address this, we redesigned the shelves to look more realistic and organized them by departments such as bakery, pantry, and dairy.
**_Change 2:_** We received feedback during our playtesting that our disability representation should also include text confusion. In response, we added product labels with slightly blurred text to better reflect this experience.
**_Change 3:_** We received feedback during our playtesting that there was a lack of response after selecting a product. To improve this, we added a cart feature where players can review the items they have collected, as well as a checkout screen that shows what was successfully collected and provides a final review.
**Post-Showcase:** Two planned improvements

1. Make our items more blurry or incorporate different qualities specific to the items to make it harder to notice the differences between items
2. Add a Magnify glass mechanic that takes time and uses your time when you check it
